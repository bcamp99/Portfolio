**Honey Part 1 by BEC (October 2022)**

From the moment I met him he was honey<br>A taste so sweet I had to have more<br>I wanted to pour him into me<br>Like I pour honey into my morning tea<br>He enhanced the flavor of my day<br>Bringing a calm sweeping force over me<br>While I watched the sunrise over the trees<br>I wondered if this honey would ever run empty

---

**Honey Part 2 by BEC (January 2023)**

Months earlier I had watched the sunrise over the trees<br>Wondering if the sweetest honey I’d ever tasted would run out<br>Today as I sit writing, the rain pouring down around me<br>I look out at the haze, to the naked eye I can see merely feet in front of me<br>But to the mind’s eye I can see far beyond that<br>What I can see is that there is no end in sight<br>His sweetness ebbs and flows but it has yet to run out<br>I no longer worry<br>He is still as sweet as ever and just as he pours into me, I pour into him

---

**Honey Part 3 by BEC (July 2025)**

A few years ago, I stood as rain poured down around me<br>Declaring our honey would ebb and flow, but never run out<br>That just as I poured into him, he’d pour into me

But jars do empty<br>And bees do leave the hive

The garden has gone quiet now<br>Stillness is all that remains<br>The flowers have begun to wilt<br>I don’t hear the humming like I used to<br>The bees were once busy at work

I thought his sweetness would never run out<br>But a jar cannot be filled when it’s poured into a cracked one

I hadn’t noticed at first —<br>The slow drip, the quiet loss<br>The jar never refilling<br>The garden never returning to its soft, low hum

Until one day I looked back and saw<br>Sticky trails leading nowhere<br>And flower petals shriveled on the ground

I kept pouring<br>But the honey was already gone<br>And the bees had abandoned the hive
