**Safety Off** **by BEC**

The bittersweet twinge<br>That I let the bullet go through me this time.<br>I thought I’d been hit before—<br>But I’d only ever been grazed.<br>Had I known it would shred me inside,<br>Maybe I would’ve run.<br>But I knew the risks.

We stood with arms extended,<br>Each barrel aimed at the other’s chest,<br>Fingers resting on the safety,<br>Swearing our love would never wound.

For years, our arms stayed outstretched,<br>The safeties still on—<br>But time made our hands tremble.<br>We never thought to switch arms,<br>To share the burden of holding them up for so long.

One day, I saw you click yours off.<br>I dropped my gun, raised my hands.<br>I begged as your finger tightened—

Please…<br>Please…<br>You don’t have to do this…

You whispered,<br>I have to… it hurts too much.<br>I wish I could keep my arm up,<br>Safety on forever—<br>But I can’t. I’m tired.

The shot tore through me.<br>I fell,<br>You dropped your weapon,<br>Staring at me.

“It aches. I don’t want to feel like this.”<br>I closed my eyes.<br>You said, “It won’t always feel like this. The wound will heal.”<br>But you couldn’t hear me think—

I never turned my safety off.<br>No matter how heavy it got,<br>I swore I’d keep it on.

The wound is too wide now to stitch.<br>It will never truly close.

I only wish you could’ve kept your safety on.
